1.1.1 Experimental Design

Module 1: Development of Practical Skills in Biology

Overview

Experimental design is a key part of scientific practice. It involves planning an investigation so that results are reliable, repeatable, and valid.

Key Concepts

  • Independent Variable – the factor you change in the experiment.
  • Dependent Variable – the factor you measure or observe.
  • Control Variables – factors kept constant to ensure a fair test.
  • Include repeats and averages to improve reliability.

Good Experimental Practice

Always ensure experiments are designed with clear aims, accurate measurements, and proper control groups. Results should be recorded systematically and evaluated critically.

Summary:

A well-designed experiment tests one variable at a time, includes controls, and gathers reproducible quantitative data for valid conclusions.
